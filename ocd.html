What is OCD?

  • Thoughts get stuck in a loop and cannot be forgotten
  • It is a problem and NOT a part of your child's personality
  • Intrusive thoughts
  • Brain hiccups
  • Brain is stuck in danger gear and cannot move out
  • Broken fire alarm that won't stop
  • Junk Mail
  • Your child don't want to behave like this
  • Brain get's stuck in a geer
  • OCD insists on being 100% certain beyond a shadow of a doubt which is impossible
  • Rituals feel like tools of survival for the child
  • Your child sacrifices immensely for OCD with little reward
  • Compulsions can be postponed but will eventually happen like holding your breath
  • Compulsions reduce anxiety for a short amount of time
  • PANDAS: Some cases of Strep throat have shown a linkage because the body's immune system attacks a part of the brain, explains sudden onset of symptoms
  • On average there will be 4 obsessions and about 5 compulsions over the entire course of the disease
  • From a biological standpoint OCD is no different than diabetes or asthma as it is a treatable medical condition

Avoid These

  • Avoid reassuring your child about the obsession which is just playing to the OCD
  • Don't punish for OCD behavior
  • Stop listening to the OCD demands
  • Stop thinking of the idea of making the OCD disappear
  • No Caffeine, increases anxiety
  • Avoid lack of sleep
  • Avoid Excessive stress
  • Avoid Conflict
  • Avoid changes in routine
  • Ridicule, teasing, and name-calling not allowed by any family members
  • Don't focus on losses
  • Don't display inconsistent messages or behavior from both parents, work on being consistent in your message and how you handle the OCD
  • Don't compare siblings or relatives and always treasure your child's uniqueness
  • Avoid letting other relatives talk about the OCD as it may embarrass your child, have private conversations with family before gatherings if needed
  • Don't completely avoid situations that are difficult
  • Don't rescue your child from a scary situation when they have the most anxiety
  • Don't reinforce the anxiety by paying too much attention to it, positive or negative

Ritual Prevention

  • Do the ritual wrong, count wrong number of times
  • Don't do the ritual at all, walk away and delay for 10 to 15 minute intervals
  • Shorten the ritual, only tap fingers 50 times instead of 100
  • Purposely do exactly what the OCD is warning not to do
  • During 15 minute ritual delay have child rank anxiety (start, after each 5 mins), anxiety should decrease
  • Have your child say out loud for the OCD to leave them alone and to stop and BACK OFF!
  • Have your child ask themselves if they want to be doing this now
  • Have your child answer what they would rather be doing
  • Activities during prevention period: talking to friend or family, low frustration puzzles like word finds, listening to music, walking, running, dancing, PLANKING
  • Anxiety is uncomfortable but not unbearable
  • Encourage the spirit of breaking the rules of OCD but focus on what your child is able to do not what he can't

Recognize an OCD moment

  • Child insists on getting an answer now
  • Child needs to know more detail than necessary
  • Child is awkwardly avoiding something ordinary
  • Child is upset things are NOT exactly a certain way
  • Child is unable to make a decision or answer a question because he fears he will be wrong and not have the one right answer
  • Child asks you to repeate phrases over and over again

Obsessions are Thoughts

  • Intrusive: unwanted thoughts
  • Irrational: don't make sense
  • Recurrent: stuck on replay
  • Disturbing: frightening and not normal thoughts
  • Anxiety Producing: when you cannot get rid of the thoughts you feel out of control

Common Obsessions

  • Contamination
  • Harm to self or others
  • Symmetry urges
  • Doubting
  • Numbers
  • Religiosity
  • Hoarding
  • Sexual Themes

Compulsions are Actions

  • Washing or cleaning
  • Checking and rechecking
  • Symmetry like retying shoes so loops are same
  • Counting
  • Repeating or Redoing
  • Hoarding
  • Praying

Medication

  • Serotonin helps brain messages flow properly
  • SSRIs are Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors which just means they help Serotonin stick around longer and help brain messages flow better
  • Prozac, Zoloft, Paxil are common SSRIs
  • Will reduce OCD symptoms but therapy is still recommended
  • Turns down the volume
  • One third of patients response to meds
  • May take 8-10 weeks for a new med to work completely

Handling OUTBURSTS

  • When escalating goal is NOT to stop outburst
  • Goal is to SLOW it down
  • Don't add fuel to the fire
  • You want to contain the fire
  • Remember your child is doing the best he can with what he's got
  • Stop, pull back and breath
  • Approach calmly with caution
  • Contain, stop, drop, and roll
  • Don't deal with cause of fire
  • Reestablish safety by setting limit or removing child from situation
  • Talk slowly, calmly
  • Label it: It looks like you are stuck. You are having a really hard time now.
  • Give feedback label when your child is calm
  • Don't wait till eruption, nip it in the bud
  • Don't panic, leave room if you cannot be calm
  • Don't criticize or correct child
  • Don't use threats
  • Don't try to reason about the situation
  • Dont' discuss the what or why until child is calm
  • Slow down so we can talk this out
  • I know you don't want to lose your cool
  • Is this worth losing privileges over?
  • You've got ice in your veins (to cool down)
  • Put on the brakes
  • Count to 10
  • This isn't a big deal, you can handle it
  • Focus, breath
  • You are in control
  • Don't blow up it's not worth it
  • If child breaks a house rule (hitting) give him a chance to tell you he broke the rule and reiterate (Right, we don't hit we use our words)
  • Tell child if he is not able to slow it down you will help him, give him a chance to tell the OCD to back off
